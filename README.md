## 📌 Background & Overview

### 🌐 E-Commerce Platform – Business Context

The rapid growth of **online commerce** has increased the need for businesses to continuously monitor sales performance, customer purchasing behavior, and operational efficiency.

This project analyzes transactional data from an e-commerce platform selling products across three major categories: Office Supplies, Furniture, Technology

The platform serves customers across multiple regions and supports various payment methods, devices, and shipping options.

To better understand business performance, a Power BI dashboard called the **"E-Commerce Performance Hub"** was developed to provide a centralized view of key operational and sales metrics.

The analysis focuses on **Gross Merchandise Value (GMV)** as the primary metric representing total transaction value generated by the platform.


### 🎯 Business Objective

The goal of this project is to build a **comprehensive analytics dashboard** that allows stakeholders to monitor business performance and understand the key factors driving revenue.

Specifically, the dashboard helps stakeholders:

- Track **sales performance and GMV growth**
- Identify **high-performing product categories**
- Analyze **customer purchasing behavior**
- Evaluate **delivery and logistics performance**
- Understand **customer segmentation and loyalty**

By integrating these perspectives, the dashboard supports **data-driven decision making** to improve revenue growth, operational efficiency, and customer satisfaction.


### ❗ Why This Analysis Matters

In an e-commerce business, revenue performance is influenced by multiple interconnected factors including customer behavior, product mix, logistics performance, and pricing strategy.

Without a structured analysis:

- Businesses may fail to identify the **drivers behind revenue growth**
- Operational inefficiencies may remain hidden
- Customer purchasing patterns may not be fully understood
- High-value customer segments may be overlooked

This project provides a **holistic performance view**, enabling stakeholders to identify **growth opportunities, operational bottlenecks, and strategic priorities**.


### 👤 Target Users

The dashboard is designed to support several key stakeholders.

#### Senior Management
Monitor overall business performance and revenue trends.

#### E-Commerce Operations Team
Track delivery performance and fulfillment efficiency.

#### Marketing & Growth Teams
Analyze customer purchasing behavior and device usage.

#### Business / Data Analysts
Explore sales drivers and identify improvement opportunities.


### ❓ Key Business Questions

The dashboard addresses several important business questions across four analytical areas.

#### Business Performance
- How is **GMV evolving over time**?
- What is the relationship between **orders and revenue growth**?

- Which **product categories contribute most to GMV**?

- **Source:** Internal company dataset

## 📊 Key Insights & Visualizations

### 🔍 Dashboard Preview

### I. Business Overview

<img width="1350" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/8679337e-4d21-419e-9e3e-4ca3e77c5e15" />

### 📌 Key Findings:

#### 1. Overall Business Performance  

Total **GMV reached 47.9M** with **108K successful orders**, generating an **Average Order Value (AOV) of 422.9**.  
However, **SLA Compliance is relatively low at 43.09%**, while **Repeat Purchase Rate is high at 68.06%**.  
→ The business shows **strong customer retention and transaction volume**, but **logistics performance may be a bottleneck affecting service quality**.

#### 2. GMV & Orders Trend Over Time  

**GMV and Successful Orders** remained relatively stable from **2021 to mid-2022**, followed by a **sharp increase in orders in Q4 2022**, while GMV slightly declined.  
→ The spike in orders without proportional GMV growth suggests **smaller transaction sizes** or **promotional activities driving order volume**.

#### 3. Product Category Performance  
**Office Supplies generated the highest GMV (~29M)** with a stable **Gross Profit Margin (~15.97%)**, significantly outperforming **Furniture (~10M)** and **Technology (~9M)**.  
→ **Office Supplies is the primary revenue driver**, contributing the largest share of sales with **consistent profitability**.

#### 4. Payment Method Distribution  

**Credit Card dominates with 54.84% of GMV**, followed by **Cash on Delivery (30.1%)**.  
Meanwhile, **E-Wallet (10.01%)** and **Bank Transfer (5.05%)** account for smaller shares.  
→ The business relies heavily on **card-based payments**, indicating strong **digital payment adoption**, but **limited diversification in payment channels**.

#### 5. Device-based Shopping Behavior  

**Mobile contributes the largest GMV (~29M)**, significantly higher than **Desktop (~14M)** and other devices.  
Average discount rates remain **relatively consistent across devices (~4.8%–5%)**.  
→ **Mobile is the dominant shopping channel**, suggesting a **mobile-first strategy** is essential for future growth.

#### 6. Regional Revenue & Customer Loyalty  

**West leads GMV (~15.5M)** followed by **East (~13.6M)** and **Central (~11.1M)**.  
However, **Repeat Purchase Rate is highest in the South (~193.5%)**, despite lower GMV contribution.  
→ Some regions drive **revenue scale**, while others demonstrate **strong customer loyalty**, indicating opportunities for **region-specific growth strategies**.

#### 7. Shipping Method Performance  

**Standard Class handles the largest order volume (~64.7K)** compared to **Second Class (~21.2K)** and **First Class (~16.5K)**.  
Premium shipping options such as **Same Day (~5.9K orders)** account for a much smaller share.  
→ **Standard shipping remains the operational backbone**, while premium shipping options serve **smaller but potentially higher-value customer segments**.


### II. Shopping Behavior

<img width="1350" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ef6792a8-e401-454d-80f8-4edc9031886a" />

### 📌 Key Findings:

#### 1. Customer Behavior Overview  

Total **GMV remains at 47.9M** with **108K successful orders**, while **Repeat Purchase Rate reaches 68.06%**.  
Customers purchase again on average after **116.6 days (ATBP)**, with a **Gross Profit Margin of 15.97%** and **Average Discount Rate of ~4.99%**.
→ The business maintains **strong customer retention and stable profitability**, while discounts remain **well controlled**.

#### 2. Orders Trend by Customer Tier  

**Successful orders peaked around ~14.9K in late 2021**, then gradually declined before dropping sharply to **~8.83K in Q4 2022**.  
Across the entire period, **Loyal customers consistently contributed the largest share of GMV** compared to **New** and **Repeat** segments.
→ **Loyal customers are the primary revenue driver**, highlighting the importance of **customer retention strategies**.

#### 3. Device-based Customer Behavior & Discount Impact  

**Mobile generated the highest GMV (~15M+)**, followed by **Desktop (~8M)**, while **Game Console recorded the lowest GMV (~1–2M)**.  
Average discount rates across devices remained **stable around ~4.8%–5.0%**.
→ **Mobile dominates customer purchasing behavior**, indicating a **strong mobile-first commerce pattern**.

#### 4. Payment Method Efficiency (GMV vs Discount Rate)  

**Credit Card contributes the highest GMV (~26M)** with a **moderate discount rate (~5.0%)**.  
**Cash on Delivery generates ~14M GMV**, while **E-Wallet and Bank Transfer contribute significantly lower revenue**.
→ **Credit Card payments are the most valuable channel**, balancing **high revenue contribution with manageable discount costs**.

#### 5. GMV Distribution by Device Type  

**Mobile accounts for the largest GMV distribution**, significantly exceeding **Desktop**, while **Tablet and Game Console represent minor revenue sources**.
→ Revenue is **highly concentrated in mobile commerce**, suggesting optimization opportunities in **mobile UX, app performance, and mobile marketing**.

#### 6. Customer Segment Value Analysis  

Among customer segments:  
- **Corporate generates the highest GMV (~12.14M)**  
- **Small Business (~11.92M)** and **Home Office (~11.89M)** contribute similar revenue levels  
All segments show **similar AOV (~418–423)** and **repeat rates (~0.88)**, with **gross profit margins around ~16%**.
→ Revenue is **balanced across customer segments**, indicating **diversified customer value without over-reliance on a single segment**.


### III. Delivery Performance

<img width="1350" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/707c6cf5-dc7a-4941-a1ae-774679c8019f" />

### 📌 Key Findings:

#### 1. Delivery Performance Overview  

Total **GMV from successful deliveries reached 45.76M**, generated from **108K successful orders**.  
The **Average Delivery Time is 3.46 days**, while **Cancellation Rate remains relatively low at 4.32%**.  
However, the **Late Delivery Rate is extremely high at 57.26%**, resulting in **SLA Compliance of only 43.09%**.
→ Although revenue realization is strong, **delivery reliability is the major operational weakness**.

#### 2. GMV vs Delivery Performance Trend  

Quarterly **GMV remained relatively stable between ~5.3M and ~7.0M** from **2021–2022**.  
Meanwhile, **Late Delivery Rate increased significantly toward late 2022**, while **Cancellation Rate stayed stable (~4–5%)**.
→ Revenue growth appears **largely independent of delivery delays**, suggesting **logistics inefficiencies have not yet impacted demand but may threaten long-term customer satisfaction**.

#### 3. Impact of Shipping Method on GMV & SLA  

**Standard Class generated the largest GMV (~28.13M)**, far exceeding **Second Class (~8.68M)** and **First Class (~6.47M)**.  
However, **SLA Compliance is much higher for Same Day shipping**, despite generating the **lowest GMV (~2.49M)**.
→ **Standard shipping drives scale**, while **faster shipping methods deliver better reliability but lower revenue contribution**.

#### 4. Impact of Shipping Status on Orders & GMV  

**Late deliveries dominate with ~62K orders**, generating **~25.26M GMV**.  
Other statuses include **Advance Shipping (~25.9K orders)** and **On-Time Shipping (~20.4K orders)**, while **Canceled shipments account for the smallest share (~2.14M GMV)**.
→ A **large proportion of revenue is associated with delayed deliveries**, indicating **systemic logistics inefficiencies**.

#### 5. Geographic Delivery Distribution  

Delivery activity is **widely distributed across the U.S.**, with several regions showing **high order concentrations and large delivery volumes**.
→ Regional distribution suggests opportunities to **optimize logistics networks and warehouse placement to reduce delivery delays**.

#### 6. Quarterly Delivery Performance (Q1 2021 – Q4 2022)

From **Q1 2021 to Q4 2022**, the detailed matrix reveals several patterns:
- **GMV steadily increased from 5.64M (Q1 2021) to 7.20M (Q4 2022)**.
- **Total Orders peaked around ~15.5K in 2021**, before dropping significantly to **~9.2K in Q4 2022**.
- **Cancellation Rate remained stable between ~4.1%–4.7%**, indicating consistent order management.
- **Late Delivery Rate remained persistently high (~54%–58%) across all quarters**.
- **Average Delivery Time remained stable around ~3.38–3.53 days**.
→ The logistics system shows **stable delivery speed but consistently poor on-time performance**, indicating **structural operational delays rather than temporary disruptions**.

#### 7. Key Operational Insight  

Despite **stable revenue growth and controlled cancellation rates**, the **persistently high Late Delivery Rate (>54%)** significantly lowers **SLA compliance (~43%)**.
→ **Improving on-time delivery performance represents the largest operational opportunity to enhance customer experience and retention**.


### IV. Customer Segmentation

<img width="1350" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/31479c26-f97e-4df8-af32-71d8e3909fe5" />

### 📌 Key Findings:

#### 1. Customer Segmentation Overview  

Total **GMV reached 47.9M** generated from **108K successful orders** across **42.05K customers**.  
**Loyal customers contribute the majority of revenue with 27.03M GMV**, while the average **Frequency is 2.69 purchases per customer** and **Average Monetary value reaches ~1.14K**.
→ The business strongly depends on **loyal and repeat customers to sustain revenue growth**.

#### 2. GMV Trend by Customer Segment  

Across **2021–2022**, **Potential and Loyal segments show steady GMV growth**, while **At Risk and Churned segments remain relatively small contributors**.
By **Q4 2022**, the **Potential segment reaches the highest GMV (~4.5M)** followed by **Loyal (~2.1M)**.
→ **Potential customers represent the largest growth opportunity**, while **loyal customers provide stable revenue foundations**.

#### 3. Average Order Value by Segment  

**Potential customers generate the highest GMV (~25.96M)** with **AOV ~518.96**, significantly higher than other segments.  
Meanwhile:
- **Loyal:** ~13.97M GMV with **AOV ~442.99**  
- **At Risk:** ~4.31M GMV with **AOV ~255.24**  
- **Churned:** ~1.52M GMV with **AOV ~153.99**
→ Higher-value transactions are concentrated in **Potential and Loyal segments**, while **At Risk and Churned customers show significantly lower spending power**.

#### 4. Buying Behavior Impact on GMV  

Customer behavior segments contribute GMV as follows:
- **Engaged Buyers:** ~20.18M  
- **Loyal Buyers:** ~14.27M  
- **Returning Buyers:** ~5.93M  
- **Single Buyers:** ~5.38M  
→ **Engaged and Loyal buyers together drive the majority of revenue**, highlighting the importance of **customer engagement and retention strategies**.

#### 5. Recency vs GMV Relationship  

The scatter plot shows that **higher GMV customers tend to have lower recency values** (i.e., they purchased more recently).  
Customers with **high recency values (>1600 days)** typically generate **very low GMV**.
→ **Customer inactivity strongly correlates with declining revenue contribution**, indicating the need for **re-engagement campaigns for dormant customers**.

#### 6. RFM Customer Detail Analysis  

The RFM table reveals that many **high-GMV customers fall into the "At Risk" segment**, despite having:
- **High order frequency (300+ orders)**  
- **Strong GMV contribution (~0.15M–0.17M per customer)**  
- **Healthy profit margins (~14–16%)**
→ Several **valuable customers are at risk of churn**, representing a **critical opportunity for retention initiatives**.

#### 7. Key Customer Insight  

Although **Potential customers generate the highest total GMV**, the business relies heavily on **Loyal and Engaged buyers for consistent revenue flow**.
→ **Customer retention and engagement programs will have the greatest impact on long-term GMV growth**.
